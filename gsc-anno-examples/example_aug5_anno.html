MITALY Ferragosto-ish BBQ📅↑
When: Aug 13 from 5:30 PM – 9:00 PM
Where: Ashdown courtyard
Contact: mgraff@mit.edu
URL: https://forms.gle/jQUMRudoBCSYDSnW7

  • has roots in the ancient Roman festival of Feriae Augusti, introduced by Emperor Augustus in 18 BC.
  • Traditionally, Italians celebrate Ferragosto with family gatherings, picnics, barbecues, and fireworks,
  • often taking the opportunity to enjoy a vacation at the beach or in the mountains.

Volunteers Needed - MIT English for Speakers of other Languages Program for Service Employees (ESOL)📅↑
When: Custom schedule, 1 hour per week; 7 days a week, 9 AM–midnight
Where: MIT classrooms
Contact: esl-admins@mit.edu
URL: https://forms.gle/YzTWf9ZUYCaRincWA
  • The MIT English for Speakers of other Languages Program for Service Employees is looking for volunteers to tutor night shift employees for 1 hr/wk
  • No experience is required, just a good command of English and an interest in helping individuals whose first language is not English
  • The goals of the program are to build student’s confidence, help them become more productive in their jobs, and open up future career opportunities
  • Sundays–Thursdays from 11:00 PM–midnight on main campus OR Mon – Fri between 9am – 3pm on west side of campus
  • This is an opportunity to make a real and positive difference. Please fill sign-up via the link above. Contact esl-admins@mit.edu for details
Application Period for Fall 2024 Kaufman Teaching Certificate Program📅↑
When: Aug 1 – 18
Contact: mmangino@mit.edu
URL: https://tll.mit.edu/event/fa24-ktcp/
  • An interactive, semester-long workshop series for grad students/postdocs interested in academic careers or developing skills for their teaching @ MIT
  • Schedule: eight practice-based workshops, 2 micro-teaching sessions & an exploratory week.
  • Topics: course design, lesson planning, assessments, student feedback, inclusive classrooms. For more information, go to: https://bit.ly/4cXTgMn
  • Participants will receive a certificate and letter from the Vice Chancellor upon completing all program requirements.
  • Applications must be received by 11:59 pm (ET) on August 18. Priority is given to applicants who have fewer future opportunities to participate.

Application Window for the Fall 2024 Lesson Planning Track Opens August 19📅↑
When: Aug 19 – 25
Contact: mmangino@mit.edu
URL: https://tll.mit.edu/event/fa24-lpt/
  • Applications open at 12:00 noon ET on Monday, August 19, and close at 11:59 pm Sunday, August 25. Open to graduate students only.
  • Participants are selected on a first-come, first-served basis with priority given to those who have completed a prior workshop track.
  • LPT is a series of 3 workshops focused on developing effective classroom activities & presentation skills, and giving formative feedback to students.
  • Workshops are in person and will be held on Wednesdays, 9/11, 9/18, & 9/25 from 2-4 pm
  • LPT is one of 4 TLL Grad teaching tracks. Completion of all 4 tracks earns a teaching certificate. For more info, go to https://bit.ly/3L6l4SD
